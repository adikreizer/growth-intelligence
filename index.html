Primary insight — Gong

"Gong has two user populations. The Accidental Champions — CSMs, AMs, RevOps — discovered the product through their Sales colleagues and became its biggest fans. Zero negative reviews. Love rate 58–72%. Nobody is marketing to them. The Intended Users — AEs and Sales Leadership — get value but hit friction. Gong spends ~85% of its marketing on the segment that loves it least."

Module 3 — Campaign brief (CSMs + AMs)

Angle 01 — Account handoff

"You just inherited 40 accounts. You have no history."

The most dangerous moment in CS is a rep transition. Every promise ever made, every concern ever raised, every relationship ever built — gone, unless it's in Gong. CSMs who use Gong for account handoffs describe it as institutional memory: "I can ask Gong what were the reasons for buying our solution." Nobody is running this message.

Evidence: 20+ reviews explicitly describe account handoff as primary use case across TrustRadius and G2

Angle 02 — Institutional memory

"When your best rep leaves, what stays?"

Tools depreciate. Salesforce data goes stale. Outreach sequences get ignored. Gong is the only tool that gets more valuable every day — because every call becomes intelligence your next hire inherits tomorrow. Tribal knowledge used to take years to accumulate. Gong shortened that.

Evidence: TrustRadius: "Tribal knowledge used to take years. Gong shortened that." Multiple reviews on ramping in 2 weeks vs. months

Angle 03 — The 40% revenue

"40% of your revenue lives in conversations your CS team is having right now."

Your Sales team closes the first deal. But expansion, renewal, and NRR live in post-sale conversations. Most CS teams manage accounts on memory and meeting notes. The ones hitting 120% NRR run on Gong — and can prove what was promised to every customer.

Evidence: Confirmed by Gong's own LinkedIn content + CSM reviews on expansion signal capture
